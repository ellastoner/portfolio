INTRODUCTION

The Problem

Chinoiserie wants to improve their customer engagement and sales of their food by providing table ordering via digital devices. Customers will be to use touch screens at their table or booth, tablet type devices mounted at the bar or on their phones anywhere in the venue. They require a system that enables customers to easily order.

image sourced by @chinoiserie instagram

01 OBSERVING THE VENUE | OFFLINE RESEARCH

Experiencing the venue

Before starting any idea generation, I wanted to observe the restaurant in action. I invited six people to dine with me, and had them fill out a survey after their experience. I focused on the following:

  • Seating Layout
  • Menu
  • Restaurant flow
  • Customer demographic
  • Payment method
  • Queue wait time
  • Customer experience & pain-points

image sourced by @chinoiserie instagram

04 DEFINING THE REQUIREMENTS

Product Requirements

After researching the problem the key issues were as follows:

  • Improve their customer engagement
  • Have payment built into the system
  • Make meals easily splittable
  • Have a take-away / dine-in option
  • Change the cluttered flow of the narrow restaurant

DEVICE BRAINSTORM

Built-in Table Kiosk

The chosen concept was a built-in table kiosk. Inspired by the retro table, arcade games. This enables customers to still place food and drinks on the table. The drag-and-drop would be the best interface for the system, however due to my coding limitations, I had to conceptulise a tap-to-order system instead.

05 WIREFRAMING

Lo-fi Wireframes

After analysing the possible devices that could be used as an ordering platform, I created wireframes for a kiosk that will be built into the tables of the restaurant that will save space.

06 VALIDATING USER EXPERIENCE

MVP Testing

The following problems were identified from user testing :

  • Heirarchy needs to be more promenient
  • The ‘create-user’ was confusing
  • Order list needs to be clearer
  • Needs an option to ‘send to kitchen’ as users wanted to order more later such as desserts
  • Icons need to be more obvious

07 FINAL DESIGN

What I made

After three iterations of the Sketch prototype, the hi-fi prototype was created. The final design would be built into a table.

MOCK-UP OF DEVICE

New customer flow

08 REFLECTIONS

Outcomes and Learnings

  • Don't under-estimate how long UX can take in a project, it never stops
  • Consider technical requirements before ideation
  • Testing this prototype in the restaurant would prove the effectiveness of the solution
  • Hick's law is crucial when designing a 'multiple choice' interface
