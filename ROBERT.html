INTRODUCTION

The Problem

The TVNZ OnDemand service is greatly expanding its catalogue of online only content. However, like all Video OnDemand (VOD) services, it is always a challenge to help viewers discover the great content TVNZ has available. We are looking to see suggestions on novel ways that we can help our viewers discover the breadth of our content and find new shows to fall in love with.

01 RESEARCH THE PROBLEM

Find out what we can

We utilsed the TVNZ representatives who were wandering about the venue. We obtained as much information about TVNZ OnDemand as possible. The most valuable was their target audience. Having the age range from 18-54 year-olds meant that our solution had to cater for a wide range of people.

03 SOLVING THE PROBLEM

Ideation

We decided to take Jake Knapp's approach of crazy eights with post-it notes instead of an A4 piece of paper. We only had 30 seconds to rapidly sketch each concept. The post-it notes were then were stuck on a board and pitched to TVNZ representatives, the top two concepts were then chosen.

IDEATION CONCEPT BOARD

SOME OF THE VOTED CONCEPTS

04 DECIDING FACTOR

Top Two Concepts

The top two ideas, voted by the TVNZ representatives was my idea, and Dane's. My idea was to put the control back to the user by giving them a graph that would be sectioned off into genres. Depending on the combination of what the user input into the graph, they would be presented with a custom belt of relative content. The second concept was a Tinder inspired feature where users could 'like' or 'dislike' content presented to them.

CHOSEN CONCEPT

05 PROTOTYPING

Prototyping and Presenting

The chosen concept was my graph idea. My task was to create this into a prototype for both mobile and desktop on Sketch. The other team members compiled the presentation, speech and code for the concept in preparation for presenting to the judging panel.
